How I'd point this at audit

Here's the part where I show I understand what Fieldguide does, not just that I can write the code.

An audit conclusion is a proposition. The workpaper evidence is the source. The reviewer's question never really changes: does the evidence actually support the conclusion, and is the standard cited correctly? That is Veritas, re-pointed from case law to audit evidence and the standards underneath it. Same shape every time:

  • Break the conclusion into atomic assertions.
  • Retrieve the evidence that's supposed to support each one.
  • Force a structured verdict, not prose.
  • Cap the reasoning, and never let confidence outrun the evidence.
  • Route the gray ones to a human, which in audit is not optional.
