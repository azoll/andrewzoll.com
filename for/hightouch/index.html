What your role asks for, line by line

"AI agents own the full experiment workflow: identify audience, generate variants, configure the experiment, launch, monitor significance, reallocate traffic."
This is AndrewOS. Builder, reviewer, and validator agents run the loop end to end across two businesses, and FieldCommand has four AI features live in production doing real work. I don't theorize about agents owning a workflow. I run them daily.
"The human reviews before launch."
My validator agent grades the builder and blocks bad output before anything ships, and there are explicit human gates on top. The whole design is autonomy up to the launch line, then a person says go. That gate is the product.
"A zero-to-one track record. We focus on impact and potential more than years of experience."
Zero-to-one is the only way I've ever built. Veritas, FieldCommand, and AndrewOS each went from nothing to shipped, solo. No team to inherit, no roadmap handed down. Just impact, on the board.
"Product judgment for where AI is safe to run autonomously versus where it needs a guardrail."
FieldCommand gates every AI feature behind per-tenant flags and routes work through a two-tier model router: cheap work to a cheap model, hard reasoning to a premium one, three providers wired in. Knowing what to automate and what to gate is the daily call.
"Define how you measure quality and decide ship or no-ship."
Veritas is that discipline, built and shipped: read a claim, retrieve the source, return a structured verdict with a confidence cap. I cut its false-positive rate from ~30% to under 5% by reading failures one at a time. Significance and ship-or-hold aren't new to me.
